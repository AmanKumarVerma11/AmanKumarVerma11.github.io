# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: Startup founders evaluating a potential technical co-founder or senior engineer — scanning late at night, making fast decisions about execution capability. Secondary: tech company recruiters and international freelance clients. Context: high signal-to-noise filter. They decide in under 60 seconds whether this person deserves more attention.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio that proves Aman is a different kind of developer — one who ships real products, thinks at a systems level, and has the taste to back it up. Success: a founder bookmarks the page or reaches out. Failure: they bounce without contacting.

## Brand Personality

Quietly intimidating. Precise. Proven.

Voice: speaks through the work, not about it. No hype adjectives. Short declarative statements. Everything earns its place or gets cut.

Emotional goal: the visitor should feel "this person is the real deal" — not through decoration, but through the quality of the interface itself.

## Anti-references

Generic dev portfolios: floating geometric PNG decorations, blue-500 accents, typewriter effects, card grids, blob animations, "techie mode" cyberpunk cosplay. Editorial-magazine aesthetics (display serif + italic + rules). Any design that could have been generated by a template.

## Design Principles

1. **The portfolio is the proof.** The quality of the design IS the argument. If this is how he builds his portfolio, imagine what he builds for clients.
2. **Restraint as confidence.** Every element earns its place. Decoration is noise. What remains after cutting is the signal.
3. **Show work, don't pitch it.** The projects section is evidence, not a sales brochure.
4. **One committed choice over ten safe ones.** A single bold decision signals taste. Five hedged decisions signal insecurity.
5. **Speed of comprehension.** A founder scanning quickly should grasp the value proposition in one fold.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. prefers-reduced-motion respected. Sufficient contrast on all text at all sizes.
